Pre-submission 3 work

Through a unified consensus in the group, we decided to create a turn-based game called Incursion. The unique selling point of the game was to place a modern multiplayer twist on the genre so people could play and strategize together to defeat a common foe (this also satisfied the specification requirements). Incursion is a game about restoring human rule over territories which have been hijacked by Orcs. Both players will work together to work up the ranks with each time conquering a more formidable opponent. While progressing, they will improve their deck and become stronger in preparations for the final battle.
In order to achieve our objectives and provide some deliverables for each submission, we divided into teams which would focus on a specific task in the submission, these teams differed and changed through the course of the work with extra knowledge of team members strengths and weaknesses, this combined with weekly meetings allowed for us to successfully:
• Create a Hack
• Create a website (with custom art)
• File documentation
With further meetings with the Critic and the Advisors, we were ready to once again reshuffle so we could better achieve our targets for the 3rd submission.

Submission 3 work

Within submission 3, there are far less deliverables which allowed us to collaborate more on the work. To profit from this, we split ourselves into groups based upon ability and time management for certain members. The teams were to be focused on Programming and Website. This insured that more people could adapt to one goal encouraging communication and team cohesion. The teams communicated through Whatsapp Group chats and Discord servers.

Update 3.2

After researching and attempting to continue with our plans, the Programming team (Deepanker, Khalid and Robbie) have hit snags in their development, this was because the programming source LibGDX was becoming far too difficult and tedious to uphold all the work they aimed to do, as a result of this the team is now searching for a new method of game development, stay tuned for updates!

Success! The programming team have successfully made the switch from LibGDX to Unity in an extra effort to get their projects done on time. This change will bring many advancements are we can now also brainstorm new ideas for future additions and further improve the game for you. Look forward to more updates.
